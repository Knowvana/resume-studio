Mastering Resume Architect

A comprehensive guide to crafting world-class resumes using our studio tools. Learn how to edit, customize, and export your professional story.

The Basics

Resume Architect Studio is a "What You See Is What You Get" (WYSIWYG) editor. There are no complex forms to fill out separately. You edit your resume directly on the paper layout.

  • Click to Edit: Simply click on any text (Name, Role, Description) to start typing.
  • Auto-Save: Your work is automatically saved to your browser's local storage.
  • Rich Text: formatting toolbars appear when you click into detailed sections (Experience, Summary).

Section Breakdown

The Sidebar (Left Column)
Profile Image

Click the round placeholder image to upload your own photo. For best results, use a square professional headshot.

Contact Details

Edit your Location, Phone, Email, and LinkedIn URL. Use standard formats (e.g., "New York, NY").

Certifications

Click the (+) button to add new certs. Click the icon next to a cert to change its FontAwesome icon class.

Core Competencies

Organize skills by category. Click the Dots to set your proficiency level (1-5).

Main Content (Right Column)
Professional Profile

A rich-text area for your executive summary. Use the formatting toolbar to add bold keywords or italics. The "Impact Statement" below it is for your unique value proposition quote.

Experience & Timeline

Click "+ Role" to add a job.
Details The description box supports bullet points. Press Enter to create new bullets.
Dates Use the date pickers. Check "Currently Working" to automatically set the end date to "Present".

Signature Achievements

Distinct cards to highlight major wins. Great for metrics (e.g., "Saved $1M", "Led 50+ Team"). You can change the icons by clicking on them.

Finalizing Your Resume

Design & Theme

Click the floating Paint Brush icon (bottom right) to open the Theme Panel.

  • Change Sidebar Color (Navy, Slate, Green, Red).
  • Switch Typography (Lato, Roboto, Serif).
Export to PDF

Press Ctrl + P (or Cmd + P on Mac) to print.

Pro Tip: Set paper size to A4 and margins to None / Minimum in your browser print settings for the best layout.
